Understanding Your Results

{% if r.result == "Normal Color Vision" %}

You correctly identified symbols across all CVD types and difficulty levels. The control plate confirmed your display is functioning, and confusion plates did not mislead you. This indicates typical trichromatic color vision.

{% elif "protanopia" in r.result.lower() %}

Protanopia affects L-cones (red-sensitive photoreceptors). Reds appear darker and are confused with greens and browns. Affects approximately 1.3% of males and 0.02% of females.

{% elif "deuteranopia" in r.result.lower() %}

Deuteranopia affects M-cones (green-sensitive photoreceptors). This is the most common form of CVD (~5% of males). Greens appear brownish and are easily confused with reds.

{% elif "tritanopia" in r.result.lower() %}

Tritanopia affects S-cones (blue-sensitive photoreceptors). Very rare (~0.01%). Blues appear greenish and yellows pinkish or light gray.

{% elif "achromatopsia" in r.result.lower() %}

Achromatopsia (rod monochromacy) is total color blindness. Extremely rare (~0.003%). Only luminance differences are perceived — the world appears entirely in grayscale.

{% endif %} {% if r.severity == "mild" %}
