Color Vision Deficiency

Understanding the spectrum

Over 300 million people experience some form of CVD worldwide — 1 in 12 males are affected.

01

Protanopia

Red-blind · L-cone absent
1.3%males

Red appears dark or brownish, making red-green distinctions nearly impossible. Traffic lights and warning signals are significantly affected.

26% of CVD cases
02

Deuteranopia

Green-blind · M-cone absent
5.0%males

The most common CVD. Green shades appear brownish-tan and are confused with reds. UI success/error states are frequently indistinguishable.

Most common type
03

Tritanopia

Blue-blind · S-cone absent
0.01%all

Blue appears greenish and yellow looks pink or light gray. Very rare. Blue-yellow pairings in UIs are safe for red-green CVD but fail here.

6% of CVD cases
04

Achromatopsia

Total color blind · No cones
0.003%all

Complete absence of color perception. Only luminance differences remain. Every hue collapses into a shade of gray — the world is entirely monochrome.

Rarest form
